JESUS-CENTRED CHRISTIAN HOLIDAY GUIDE
For the 22 Discord banner images previously created
Prepared for server-event reminders and devotional room use

Scope note:
These dates are given for the Western Christian calendar in 2026, with reusable annual rules where helpful. Some traditions observe or transfer dates differently, especially Eastern Orthodox churches and local Roman Catholic, Anglican, Lutheran, Methodist, and Reformed/Presbyterian calendars. Several items below are more strongly Catholic, Anglican, or Lutheran than Presbyterian, so “required” should be read as “customary, recommended, or devotionally fitting,” not as a universal command upon all Christians.

Scripture note:
The NASB 2020 is the current New American Standard Bible edition from The Lockman Foundation. To avoid over-quoting copyrighted Bible text, this guide gives suggested NASB 2020 references and careful summaries rather than long direct quotations. For exact wording, read from an authorised NASB 2020 Bible or authorised NASB source.

============================================================
01. ADVENT
2026 date: Begins Sunday, 29 November 2026; continues until Christmas Eve, 24 December 2026.
Annual rule: Begins on the fourth Sunday before Christmas, usually between 27 November and 3 December.
Suggested NASB 2020 readings: Isaiah 9:2-7; Luke 1:26-38; Matthew 1:18-25; Mark 13:33-37.

Summary of what is required or fitting:
Prepare the heart for Christ through prayer, Scripture, repentance, charity, patient hope, and reverent expectation. Many churches use Advent candles, hymns, readings, and weekly themes such as hope, peace, joy, and love.

Long explanation:
Advent is not merely the countdown to Christmas; it is a season of disciplined waiting. It looks in two directions at once: backward toward the birth of Christ in Bethlehem, and forward toward His promised return in glory. Its tone is therefore both tender and serious. It invites Christians to ask whether their lives are ready to receive the Lord: not only in sentiment, but in conduct, mercy, humility, and truth.

For a Discord reminder or worship-room post, Advent can be framed as the season in which the soul trims its lamp. The day does not require noise, spectacle, or spiritual performance. It asks for watchfulness, kindness, and preparation. A fitting observance would be to read a prophetic passage, pray quietly, forgive someone if possible, give to the needy, or set aside a habit that clouds the mind. Advent honours holy longing: the world is dark, yet the Light is coming.

02. ANNUNCIATION
2026 date: Wednesday, 25 March 2026.
Annual rule: 25 March, nine months before Christmas; sometimes transferred if it conflicts with Holy Week or Easter observances.
Suggested NASB 2020 readings: Luke 1:26-38; Isaiah 7:14; John 1:14; Galatians 4:4-7.

Summary of what is required or fitting:
Remember the angelic announcement to Mary and the beginning of the Incarnation. Pray with humility, honour obedient faith, and reflect on the Word becoming flesh.

Long explanation:
The Annunciation marks the moment when the promise of redemption enters human history in a hidden and astonishing way. The Christian faith does not begin with an abstract theory, but with God drawing near through a real human life. Mary’s answer is therefore remembered not as passive silence, but as faithful consent before a mystery too large for ordinary understanding.

For a server or devotional setting, this feast is ideal for reflecting on vocation, obedience, and trust. It asks believers to consider whether they would accept God’s call even when the path is unclear. It also reminds the community that great divine works often begin quietly, before the world notices. A fitting observance is prayer, a reading of Luke 1, gratitude for Christ’s humanity, and kindness toward those carrying heavy responsibilities in silence.

============================================================
03. CHRISTMAS
2026 date: Friday, 25 December 2026.
Annual rule: 25 December in Western Christianity.
Suggested NASB 2020 readings: Luke 2:1-20; Matthew 1:18-25; John 1:1-18; Isaiah 9:2-7.

Summary of what is required or fitting:
Celebrate the Nativity of Jesus Christ with worship, gratitude, generosity, family peace where possible, and remembrance of the poor and lonely.

Long explanation:
Christmas celebrates the birth of Jesus Christ, the mystery that the eternal Son of God entered the world in humility rather than domination. Its power lies in contrast: heaven appears through a child, glory is hidden in vulnerability, and divine kingship is revealed through poverty, simplicity, and peace. The manger is therefore not merely sentimental; it is a theological declaration about the way God chooses to come near.

A fitting observance includes worship, Scripture, prayer, and acts of generosity. The day should not be reduced to consumption, nor should it become a burden of social performance. At its best, Christmas calls people to welcome Christ by welcoming mercy, reconciliation, and hospitality. In a Discord room, Christmas can be described as the day when the Light enters the human story, not as an escape from sorrow, but as God’s answer within it.

04. HOLY NAME OF JESUS
2026 date: Saturday, 3 January 2026 in many Roman Catholic calendars; some traditions connect it with 1 January or the circumcision/naming of Christ.
Annual rule: Often 3 January in the modern Roman calendar; observance varies.
Suggested NASB 2020 readings: Luke 2:21; Philippians 2:9-11; Acts 4:12; Matthew 1:21.

Summary of what is required or fitting:
Reverence the name of Jesus, avoid using it lightly or contemptuously, and reflect on the meaning of His saving identity.

Long explanation:
The Holy Name of Jesus honours the name given to the child according to divine instruction. In Scripture, a name often reveals identity, mission, and authority. The name of Jesus is not a decorative religious label; it is bound to the confession that God saves through Him. This feast therefore encourages reverence in speech, prayer, worship, and public conversation.

For server use, the day can be tied directly to conduct. Members may disagree, ask questions, or debate theology, but they should not treat the name of Christ as a weapon, joke, insult, or curse. A fitting observance is a short prayer in the name of Jesus, a reading from Philippians 2, or a commitment to speak sacred things with restraint and honour. The day teaches that language itself can be worshipful or profane.

============================================================
05. EPIPHANY
2026 date: Tuesday, 6 January 2026; in some places transferred to Sunday, 4 January 2026.
Annual rule: 6 January in the Western calendar, with transfers in some jurisdictions.
Suggested NASB 2020 readings: Matthew 2:1-12; Isaiah 60:1-6; Psalm 72; John 1:9-14.

Summary of what is required or fitting:
Celebrate the revelation of Christ to the nations, symbolised by the Magi. Practice hospitality, humility, and openness to God’s light beyond one’s own tribe.

Long explanation:
Epiphany means manifestation or showing forth. In Western Christianity it especially recalls the Magi coming to honour the Christ-child. The feast declares that Jesus is not the possession of one ethnic group, nation, class, or private circle. The light of Christ is shown to the nations, and the wise must bow before a wisdom greater than their own.

For a Discord event, Epiphany is especially suitable for an interfaith-respect tone while remaining clearly Christian. It can remind members that truth is not served by arrogance. The Magi travel, search, discern, worship, and give. Their journey becomes a pattern for seekers: follow the light, bring what you have, and do not return to the same old corrupt powers. A fitting observance is prayer for all nations, kindness to strangers, and gratitude for the guidance God gives through Scripture, conscience, creation, and providence.

============================================================
06. BAPTISM OF THE LORD
2026 date: Sunday, 11 January 2026 in many Western calendars.
Annual rule: Usually the Sunday after Epiphany, though rules vary by tradition and transfer.
Suggested NASB 2020 readings: Matthew 3:13-17; Mark 1:9-11; Luke 3:21-22; John 1:29-34.

Summary of what is required or fitting:
Remember Jesus’ baptism in the Jordan, renew baptismal gratitude where applicable, and honour the revelation of Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.

Long explanation:
The Baptism of the Lord marks the beginning of Christ’s public ministry and reveals the Trinity with remarkable clarity: the Son stands in the water, the Spirit descends, and the Father’s voice declares divine pleasure. Jesus does not enter the Jordan because He needs repentance; He enters in solidarity with sinners and begins the path that leads toward the Cross.

A fitting observance is to remember one’s own baptism, pray for renewal, and recommit to walking as a child of God. For a server room, this celebration strongly supports respectful Trinitarian discussion. It reminds members that Christian faith is not merely moral advice, but communion with the Father through the Son in the Holy Spirit. The day also carries a humble lesson: Christ begins His mission not by exalting Himself before crowds, but by stepping into the waters of human need.

============================================================
07. PRESENTATION OF THE LORD / CANDLEMAS
2026 date: Monday, 2 February 2026.
Annual rule: 2 February, forty days after Christmas.
Suggested NASB 2020 readings: Luke 2:22-40; Malachi 3:1-4; Hebrews 2:14-18.

Summary of what is required or fitting:
Remember Jesus being presented in the temple. Pray for purified sight, dedicate one’s life to God, and honour Christ as light for revelation.

Long explanation:
The Presentation joins tenderness and prophecy. Mary and Joseph bring Jesus to the temple in obedience, and there Simeon and Anna recognise in the child the fulfilment of God’s promise. The old covenant and the new meet in worship, patience, and praise. It is a feast of recognition: many saw a child, but Simeon saw salvation.

In practical observance, Christians may light candles, pray for spiritual clarity, or read Luke 2 slowly. The day is especially meaningful for older believers, patient servants, parents, and those who wait long for consolation. It teaches that holy fulfilment may arrive quietly, not with worldly applause, but with a faithful heart perceiving what God has done. In a Discord setting, the day can be used to encourage patience, reverence for elders, and the dedication of one’s work and speech to God.

============================================================
08. LENT BEGINS / ASH WEDNESDAY
2026 date: Wednesday, 18 February 2026.
Annual rule: Forty-six days before Easter Sunday, counting forty fasting days plus Sundays.
Suggested NASB 2020 readings: Joel 2:12-17; Psalm 51; Matthew 6:1-18; 2 Corinthians 5:20-6:10.

Summary of what is required or fitting:
Begin a season of repentance, prayer, fasting, almsgiving, self-examination, and turning from sin toward God.

Long explanation:
Lent begins with the truth that human beings are mortal, morally wounded, and dependent upon mercy. Ash Wednesday does not exist to humiliate people theatrically, but to awaken them honestly. It says: do not pretend you are infinite; do not hide from your conscience; do not waste the life you have been given. Its sobriety is medicinal, not despairing.

The fitting observance is repentance without exhibitionism. Christ warns against practicing righteousness merely to be noticed, so fasting, prayer, and giving should be sincere rather than performative. For a server room, Lent can encourage members to reduce cruelty, gossip, mockery, addiction, and needless argument. It is not only about giving up chocolate or alcohol; it is about recovering spiritual clarity. Lent trains the will so that the heart may be freer for Easter joy.

============================================================
09. PALM SUNDAY
2026 date: Sunday, 29 March 2026.
Annual rule: The Sunday before Easter.
Suggested NASB 2020 readings: Matthew 21:1-11; John 12:12-19; Psalm 118; Philippians 2:5-11.

Summary of what is required or fitting:
Remember Christ’s entry into Jerusalem, honour Him as King, and prepare for Holy Week with humility rather than shallow excitement.

Long explanation:
Palm Sunday begins in acclamation and moves toward the Passion. The crowds welcome Jesus with royal language, yet the week will reveal how quickly human enthusiasm can collapse under fear, politics, and disappointment. This day therefore warns against a faith that praises Christ only when He seems useful or triumphant.

A fitting observance is to read the Gospel account, attend worship where possible, and begin Holy Week with seriousness. The palm branch is not merely a decoration; it is a sign that Christ’s kingship is humble, prophetic, and costly. For Discord use, Palm Sunday can be a reminder not to confuse popularity with truth. The same world that applauds righteousness may reject it when it becomes inconvenient. The day calls believers to follow Christ not only into celebration, but into obedience.

============================================================
10. MAUNDY THURSDAY / HOLY THURSDAY
2026 date: Thursday, 2 April 2026.
Annual rule: The Thursday before Easter.
Suggested NASB 2020 readings: John 13:1-17, 34-35; Luke 22:14-20; 1 Corinthians 11:23-26.

Summary of what is required or fitting:
Remember the Last Supper, Christ’s commandment of love, the institution of the Lord’s Supper, and the servant humility shown in the washing of feet.

Long explanation:
“Maundy” comes from the commandment of love. On this night, Jesus shares the meal, gives Himself sacramentally, washes feet, and prepares to be betrayed. The glory of the Lord appears as service. The teacher stoops, the master becomes servant, and the disciples are commanded to love one another in a way that reflects Him.

A fitting observance includes communion where one’s church offers it, prayer, reconciliation, and an act of humble service. The day should convict religious pride. It is not enough to speak of Christ while despising the people He calls one to love. For a server room, Maundy Thursday is an ideal day to remind members that theology without charity becomes noise. Reverence for the Lord’s Table must overflow into gentleness toward the neighbour.

============================================================
11. GOOD FRIDAY
2026 date: Friday, 3 April 2026.
Annual rule: The Friday before Easter.
Suggested NASB 2020 readings: John 18-19; Isaiah 52:13-53:12; Psalm 22; 1 Peter 2:21-25.

Summary of what is required or fitting:
Keep solemn remembrance of Christ’s crucifixion. Pray, read the Passion, avoid mockery and spiritual frivolity, and contemplate sacrificial love.

Long explanation:
Good Friday is called “good” not because suffering is good in itself, but because the Cross becomes the place where divine love confronts sin, violence, shame, and death. It is the most solemn day in the Christian year. The righteous one is condemned, the innocent suffers, and human injustice is exposed before God.

The fitting observance is quietness, Scripture, prayer, and restraint. Many Christians fast or abstain from meat, attend a Passion service, or spend time in silence. For a Discord room, this day should carry a respectful tone: no cynical jokes about the Crucifixion, no blasphemy, no using sacred suffering as entertainment. Good Friday teaches that God’s victory does not always look like immediate rescue. Sometimes love remains faithful while nailed to the world’s cruelty.

============================================================
12. HOLY SATURDAY
2026 date: Saturday, 4 April 2026.
Annual rule: The Saturday between Good Friday and Easter Sunday.
Suggested NASB 2020 readings: Matthew 27:57-66; 1 Peter 3:18-22; Romans 6:3-11; Psalm 130.

Summary of what is required or fitting:
Observe sacred waiting between the Cross and Resurrection. Practice silence, patience, grief without despair, and hope without haste.

Long explanation:
Holy Saturday is the day of the tomb. It is spiritually difficult because it offers no easy resolution. Christ has been crucified, the disciples are scattered, and the world appears to have gone silent. Yet beneath that silence, Christian hope waits. The day teaches that God’s work is not always visible to those living through the middle of sorrow.

A fitting observance is quiet prayer, restrained speech, and patience. Some churches hold the Easter Vigil after nightfall, moving from darkness to light. In a server setting, Holy Saturday can comfort those who are between endings and beginnings. It says that silence is not always abandonment, and waiting is not always failure. The tomb is real, but it is not the final word. This day protects Christians from shallow optimism by teaching holy endurance.

============================================================
13. EASTER SUNDAY
2026 date: Sunday, 5 April 2026.
Annual rule: A moveable feast; in Western Christianity it falls on the Sunday after the Paschal full moon, according to ecclesiastical calculation.
Suggested NASB 2020 readings: John 20; Luke 24; Matthew 28; 1 Corinthians 15.

Summary of what is required or fitting:
Celebrate the Resurrection of Jesus Christ with worship, joy, thanksgiving, mercy, proclamation, and renewed hope.

Long explanation:
Easter is the central feast of Christianity. Without the Resurrection, the Cross would appear only as tragedy; with the Resurrection, it becomes victory through self-giving love. Easter proclaims that death, sin, cruelty, and despair do not have the final authority. Christ is not remembered merely as a noble teacher who died; He is confessed as the risen Lord.

A fitting observance includes worship, joyful music, Scripture, fellowship, and acts of mercy. The day should lift the heart without becoming shallow. Easter joy is not denial of suffering; it is hope stronger than suffering. In a Discord community, Easter can be celebrated as the great declaration that light is not imaginary, forgiveness is possible, and history is not sealed inside the grave. It is the feast of life renewed.

============================================================
14. DIVINE MERCY SUNDAY
2026 date: Sunday, 12 April 2026.
Annual rule: The second Sunday of Easter.
Suggested NASB 2020 readings: John 20:19-31; Psalm 118; Ephesians 2:4-10; 1 John 4:7-21.

Summary of what is required or fitting:
Emphasise the mercy of the risen Christ. Practice forgiveness, confession, trust in God, and mercy toward others.

Long explanation:
Divine Mercy Sunday is especially associated with Roman Catholic devotion, but its central theme is deeply Christian: the risen Christ brings peace to fearful disciples and meets even doubting Thomas with patient truth. Mercy is not moral weakness. It is the holy strength by which God restores the wounded, forgives the repentant, and sends forgiven people into the world.

A fitting observance is prayer for mercy, reconciliation, and deliberate kindness toward someone who needs compassion. For non-Catholic Christians, the day can still be approached as a meditation on resurrection mercy. In a server room, it is a powerful reminder that religious discussion should not become merciless combat. Truth and mercy belong together. The Christ who rises from the dead does not return merely to shame His failed disciples, but to give peace and mission.

============================================================
15. ASCENSION
2026 date: Thursday, 14 May 2026 traditionally; in some places transferred to Sunday, 17 May 2026.
Annual rule: The fortieth day of Easter, traditionally a Thursday; often transferred to the following Sunday.
Suggested NASB 2020 readings: Acts 1:1-11; Luke 24:44-53; Ephesians 1:15-23; Hebrews 4:14-16.

Summary of what is required or fitting:
Remember Christ’s ascension into heaven, His reign, His priestly intercession, and the Church’s mission until His return.

Long explanation:
The Ascension is not Christ abandoning the world. It is the exaltation of the risen Lord, who reigns and intercedes. The disciples are not told to stare endlessly into the sky; they are prepared for mission. The feast therefore holds together worship and action: Christ is enthroned, and His people are sent.

A fitting observance is prayer, Scripture, and renewed commitment to Christian witness. It is also a day to reject despair about the world, because Christian hope rests not in earthly power but in the reigning Christ. For Discord use, Ascension can remind members that spiritual life is not escapism. Believers lift their minds to heaven so they may serve more faithfully on earth. The King is exalted, yet His mission continues through His people.

============================================================
16. PENTECOST
2026 date: Sunday, 24 May 2026.
Annual rule: The fiftieth day of Easter, seven weeks after Easter Sunday.
Suggested NASB 2020 readings: Acts 2:1-21; John 14:15-27; Romans 8; 1 Corinthians 12.

Summary of what is required or fitting:
Pray for the Holy Spirit, celebrate the birth or empowerment of the Church, seek unity, courage, holiness, and truthful speech.

Long explanation:
Pentecost celebrates the outpouring of the Holy Spirit upon the disciples. Fearful followers become witnesses. Divided tongues and nations are addressed by a divine message that crosses barriers. The day reverses spiritual confusion not by erasing difference, but by making the Gospel intelligible across difference.

A fitting observance includes prayer for the Spirit’s guidance, gratitude for spiritual gifts, and renewed courage to speak truth with love. For a Discord room, Pentecost strongly supports disciplined speech. Words can wound, scatter, and inflame; Spirit-filled words should clarify, heal, and witness. The day also reminds Christians that the Church is not sustained merely by organisation, personality, or argument. It lives by the breath of God, who gives courage without cruelty and conviction without hatred.

============================================================
17. CORPUS CHRISTI / BODY AND BLOOD OF CHRIST
2026 date: Thursday, 4 June 2026 traditionally; many calendars transfer it to Sunday, 7 June 2026.
Annual rule: Thursday after Trinity Sunday, often transferred to the following Sunday.
Suggested NASB 2020 readings: Luke 22:14-20; John 6:35-58; 1 Corinthians 10:16-17; 1 Corinthians 11:23-29.

Summary of what is required or fitting:
Reverence Christ’s gift in the Lord’s Supper / Eucharist. Observe according to one’s church tradition, with gratitude, humility, and self-examination.

Long explanation:
Corpus Christi is especially prominent in Roman Catholic tradition, but the subject it honours—the body and blood of Christ—is central to nearly all historic Christianity, though interpreted differently. Catholics speak of the Eucharist in one way, Reformed Christians in another, and other Protestants in still other ways. Yet the common centre remains: Christ gives Himself for His people, and the sacred meal is not to be handled carelessly.

A fitting observance depends on denomination. Catholics may attend Mass or procession; Presbyterians and other Reformed Christians may reflect on the Lord’s Supper as a true spiritual communion with Christ, received by faith. In a Discord room, this day should be handled with special respect because Eucharistic doctrine can divide Christians quickly. The recommended tone is reverent explanation, not mockery. The feast asks Christians to remember that communion with Christ must also rebuke contempt for one another.

============================================================
18. SACRED HEART OF JESUS
2026 date: Friday, 12 June 2026.
Annual rule: Friday after the second Sunday after Pentecost; nineteen days after Pentecost.
Suggested NASB 2020 readings: John 19:31-37; Matthew 11:28-30; 1 John 4:7-21; Ephesians 3:14-19.

Summary of what is required or fitting:
Meditate on the love, mercy, compassion, and self-giving heart of Christ. Practice repentance, tenderness, and charity.

Long explanation:
The Sacred Heart devotion is especially Catholic, but its core concern is broadly Christian: the love of Christ is not cold, abstract, or merely legal. The pierced side of Jesus becomes an image of divine love poured out for the world. This devotion invites the believer to contemplate not only what Christ did, but the merciful love with which He did it.

A fitting observance is prayer, gratitude, and an act of compassion. Those outside Catholic devotion can still honour the theme by meditating on the mercy and gentleness of Christ. For server use, this day can soften the room’s tone. If members discuss theology without love, they have missed the heart of the matter. The Sacred Heart points toward a faith in which truth is aflame with charity, and holiness is inseparable from mercy.

============================================================
19. TRANSFIGURATION
2026 date: Thursday, 6 August 2026.
Annual rule: 6 August in many Western calendars; also remembered liturgically near Lent in some lectionaries.
Suggested NASB 2020 readings: Matthew 17:1-8; Mark 9:2-8; Luke 9:28-36; 2 Peter 1:16-18.

Summary of what is required or fitting:
Remember Christ’s glory revealed on the mountain. Listen to Him, pray deeply, and carry the vision of glory back into ordinary obedience.

Long explanation:
The Transfiguration reveals the glory of Christ before the suffering of the Cross. Peter, James, and John see a glimpse of who Jesus truly is, accompanied by Moses and Elijah, and overshadowed by divine presence. The event is not given so the disciples can escape history, but so they may endure what is coming with deeper faith.

A fitting observance is contemplative prayer, Scripture reading, and renewed attention to Christ’s voice. The command to listen is central. For a Discord room, the Transfiguration can be used as a call to rise above shallow noise. Not every argument deserves equal reverence; not every voice deserves final authority. The Christian is called to behold Christ, then descend the mountain into service. Glory is given not for vanity, but for faithful endurance.

============================================================
20. EXALTATION OF THE HOLY CROSS
2026 date: Monday, 14 September 2026.
Annual rule: 14 September.
Suggested NASB 2020 readings: John 3:14-17; Philippians 2:5-11; 1 Corinthians 1:18-25; Galatians 6:14.

Summary of what is required or fitting:
Honour the Cross as the sign of Christ’s victory through sacrifice. Practice humility, repentance, endurance, and gratitude.

Long explanation:
The Exaltation of the Holy Cross celebrates the paradox at the centre of Christianity: the instrument of shame becomes the sign of salvation. The Cross exposes human violence and pride, yet it also reveals divine love and victory. Christians do not honour suffering for its own sake; they honour Christ, who transformed suffering through obedience and love.

A fitting observance is prayer before the Cross, reflection on humility, and willingness to bear burdens without hatred. For a server room, this feast can correct triumphalism. Christian victory is not domination over enemies; it is Christlike fidelity, even when the world misunderstands. The Cross teaches that truth may be mocked before it is vindicated. It also demands that Christians not use the symbol of the Cross as an excuse for cruelty, but as a summons to sacrificial love.

============================================================
21. CHRIST THE KING
2026 date: Sunday, 22 November 2026.
Annual rule: Last Sunday before Advent in many Western calendars.
Suggested NASB 2020 readings: Colossians 1:13-20; Luke 23:35-43; John 18:33-37; Revelation 11:15.

Summary of what is required or fitting:
Honour Jesus Christ as King and Lord over all life. Reject idolatrous power, false loyalties, cruelty, and pride.

Long explanation:
Christ the King is a comparatively recent feast in the Western calendar, but its theme is ancient: Jesus reigns. His kingship is not merely ceremonial. It judges nations, ideologies, egos, and private ambitions. Yet His throne is revealed through truth, mercy, and the Cross, not through worldly vanity.

A fitting observance is to examine what truly rules one’s life: fear, money, resentment, lust, reputation, anger, or Christ. For a Discord room, this day is excellent for a public reminder that all discussions of faith must remain beneath the lordship of Christ. He is not a mascot for private agendas. He is King. Therefore, members should speak with reverence, pursue justice, resist dehumanisation, and remember that every earthly authority is temporary before the kingdom of God.

============================================================
22. HOLY FAMILY
2026 date: Sunday, 27 December 2026 in many Western calendars.
Annual rule: Usually the Sunday within the Octave of Christmas; when rules vary, it may be observed on 30 December.
Suggested NASB 2020 readings: Luke 2:39-52; Matthew 2:13-23; Colossians 3:12-17; Ephesians 6:1-4.

Summary of what is required or fitting:
Reflect on Jesus, Mary, and Joseph as a holy household. Honour family responsibility, patience, protection of children, forgiveness, and domestic faithfulness.

Long explanation:
The Holy Family is not a sentimental picture of effortless peace. The Gospel accounts show danger, displacement, obedience, anxiety, searching, and growth. Joseph protects, Mary treasures and ponders, and Jesus grows in wisdom. The feast therefore speaks to real households, including imperfect, strained, wounded, or lonely ones. It presents family holiness as faithful love under pressure.

A fitting observance is prayer for one’s family, reconciliation where safe and wise, gratitude for caregivers, and compassion for those without stable family support. For a Discord community, the day can broaden the meaning of “family” without cheapening it: communities too should protect the vulnerable, honour children, restrain anger, and encourage maturity. The Holy Family reminds Christians that salvation entered the world through a household, and that ordinary domestic duties can become sacred acts of service.
